Infectious Diseases
════════════════════════════════════

Q: What's diarrhea, and what should I do first?
A: Diarrhea is an intestinal infection from bacteria/viruses that messes up water absorption in your gut, causing loose stools, cramps, and dehydration. It works by inflaming the gut lining, forcing out fluids rapidly. If untreated, it leads to severe weakness, organ failure, or even death from fluid loss. 

Natural first aid: Mix a homemade ORS (1 liter water + 6 tsp sugar + ½ tsp salt + lemon juice), sip slowly, rest, and skip heavy meals.

Q: Tell me about malaria.
A: Malaria comes from Plasmodium parasites spread by mosquitoes, infecting your red blood cells and causing chills and cyclic fevers. The parasite multiplies in your liver then bursts blood cells, releasing toxins. Untreated, it causes anemia, brain damage, or fatal organ failure. 

Natural first aid: Brew tulsi or ginger tea to ease fever, stay super hydrated, and use nets to block mosquitoes.

────────────────────────────────────

Q: How does dengue fever work?
A: It's a virus from Aedes mosquitoes causing high fever, killer joint pain, and rashes. The virus damages blood vessels and drops platelet counts, leading to leaks. Without treatment, you risk internal bleeding, shock, or death. 

Natural first aid: Drink coconut water or fresh papaya leaf juice to boost hydration and platelets; rest in a cool spot.

Respiratory Issues
════════════════════════════════════

Q: Explain an asthma attack.
A: Asthma is chronic airway inflammation that flares with triggers like dust, narrowing your breathing tubes. Immune chemicals swell the bronchi, blocking air. If ignored, it can cause respiratory failure or lasting lung harm. 

Natural first aid: Breathe steam from hot water with ginger slices, sit upright, and sip warm honey-lemon water.

────────────────────────────────────

Q: What's the deal with the flu (influenza)?
A: A respiratory virus that invades nose, throat, and lung cells, replicating fast to cause fever, cough, and aches. Untreated, it can spark pneumonia, especially in kids or elderly. 

Natural first aid: Make a ginger-garlic-tulsi decoction, drink lots of fluids, and get plenty of rest.

Metabolic and Heat Issues
════════════════════════════════════

Q: What is hypoglycemia (low blood sugar)?
A: Common in diabetics, it's a sudden blood glucose crash from too much insulin or missed meals, starving your brain and body. It triggers tremors and confusion. Untreated: seizures, coma, or brain damage. 

Natural first aid: If conscious, give jaggery, dates, or honey; then rest and monitor.

────────────────────────────────────

Q: How does heatstroke happen?
A: Your body overheats from heat or overexertion, failing to sweat and cool down, which cooks your proteins. Symptoms: confusion, hot dry skin. If untreated: brain swelling, organ shutdown, death. 

Natural first aid: Get to shade, sponge with cool water or ice packs on neck/armpits, sip electrolyte drinks.

Allergic and Skin Problems
════════════════════════════════════

Q: Describe anaphylaxis.
A: A massive allergic overreaction to food/stings releases histamine, swelling your throat and crashing blood pressure. It constricts airways fast. Untreated: airway block, heart stoppage. 

Natural first aid: Try turmeric milk for inflammation; lie flat with legs up if breathing's okay, seek help ASAP.

────────────────────────────────────

Q: What's a minor burn really?
A: Heat or chemicals damage skin proteins, sparking pain, redness, and blisters via inflammation. Untreated: infection or scars. 

Natural first aid: Run cool water over it for 10-20 mins, then apply fresh aloe vera gel.

────────────────────────────────────

Q: Why do I get the common cold?
A: Viruses stick to nasal cells, inflaming your upper airways for runny nose and sore throat. Untreated: it may lead to sinus or ear infections. 

Natural first aid: Sip tulsi-ginger-lemon tea, do steam inhalation, use honey for soothing.

Emergency and Trauma
════════════════════════════════════

Q: What should I do if someone is choking?
A: Choking happens when an object blocks the airway. If they can cough, encourage it. If they can't breathe/speak, perform the Heimlich maneuver (abdominal thrusts). Stand behind them, wrap arms around waist, make a fist above navel, and pull inward/upward.

Natural first aid: Ensure airway is clear once object is out; sip warm water to soothe throat.

────────────────────────────────────

Q: How to recognize a heart attack and what to do?
A: Signs include crushing chest pain, pain in left arm/jaw, and shortness of breath. It's a blockage of blood flow to the heart muscle. Untreated: permanent heart damage or death.

Natural first aid: Have the person sit and rest to reduce heart load. If they have prescribed aspirin, they can chew it (under medical advice). Call emergency services immediately.

────────────────────────────────────

Q: What is the first aid for a snake bite?
A: Snake venom can affect the nervous system or blood. Keep the victim calm and still to slow venom spread. Do NOT cut the wound or try to suck out venom. 

Natural first aid: Wash the area with soap/water, keep the bitten limb below heart level, and get to a hospital with anti-venom immediately.

────────────────────────────────────

Q: How to handle a possible bone fracture?
A: Symptoms include deformity, inability to move the limb, and intense pain. Untreated: improper healing or nerve damage.

Natural first aid: Immobilize the area using a splint (stick/board) and bandage. Apply ice to reduce swelling (not directly on skin). Do not try to realign the bone.
